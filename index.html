Towards Generative Image AI

A technical guide for the inner workings of modern generative image models

Presented by YellowPeak AI | Author: Sven Pfiffner

At its core, every generative image model shares a single goal: to learn the underlying probability distribution of visual data. How they achieve this, however, varies drastically.

This guide breaks down the core families of generative architectures. You will learn how VAEs focus on probabilistic encodings to approximate data, while GANs utilize a competitive architecture to force realism. We then bridge the gap to the latest generation of models, exploring how Diffusion models reverse the process of entropy and how Autoregressive networks treat pixels as sequences. Whether you are a developer or a researcher, this guide uncovers the inner workings of the engines driving the AI art revolution.

Prerequisites: Basic familiarity with neural networks, probability, and calculus. Each chapter is self-contained but best read in order ‚Äî later chapters build on concepts introduced earlier.
